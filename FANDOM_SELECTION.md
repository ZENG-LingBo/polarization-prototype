## 0. How to use this doc
This **narrows the field to finalists**; it does **not** lock the pair. The lock is gated on the
**CIIM-registration pilot** (§6 below). Decision owner = the team. The prototype deliberately uses
fictional placeholder fandoms (**AURORA / NOVA**) and should **keep** them — see §8; the real pair
drives the IRB protocol and recruitment, not the public demo.

---

## 1. Why the pair matters so much
The whole study rests on two facts about the chosen rivalry being *simultaneously* true:
1. There is **genuine, mutual toxicity** to reduce (the primary DV must actually exist), **and**
2. Both sides nonetheless share a **non-contested superordinate** identity ("K-pop fans") they can
   be recategorized *into*.

A pair with (1) but not (2) is Study 2 (politics — a *contested* superordinate, predicted to
backfire). A pair with (2) but not (1) has no DV to move. The right pair is the one that maximizes
**both at once** — and that is an empirical question the pilot (§6) answers.

## 3. Candidate pairs

### A. ARMY (BTS) × BLINK (BLACKPINK) — the flagship
The largest, most-documented fan war in K-pop. ARMY ≈ 40–60M, BLINK ≈ 30M+ globally.
- **Strong on:** C1 (extensively documented hostility), C4 (enormous, reasonably balanced), C7
  (huge volume of codable discourse).
- **Weak on:** C5 / directness of C1 — BTS and BLACKPINK are **different genders / different lanes**,
  so they rarely compete head-to-head on the same chart cycle; the "rivalry" is more **diffuse,
  fan-driven, and troll-amplified** than a direct competitive clash. It is also the **most public**
  rivalry in K-pop → high **demand-characteristics** risk (participants may recognize the setup and
  guess the hypothesis — a §12 threat). C3 is a question mark: many casual fans admire both.

### B. Same-lane 4th-gen boy groups — e.g. STAY (Stray Kids) × CARAT (SEVENTEEN); or Treasure × ENGENE (ENHYPEN)
Direct competitors in the **same gender, same chart cycle, same debut era**. Public reporting
describes pairs like Treasure and ENHYPEN as "constantly fighting over streams, awards, and
lightsticks."
- **Strong on:** C5 and the *directness* of C1 (head-to-head competitive toxicity over the same
  prizes), C3 (same-lane direct rivals have **lower mutual dual-stanning** than a big-two everyone
  admires), and lower **demand-characteristics** (less iconic → participants less likely to guess).
- **Weak on:** C4 — smaller (core fandoms on the order of a few hundred thousand vs. tens of
  millions), so **balanced recruitment takes more effort** and dosing throughput is lower.

### C. Same-lane 4th-gen girl groups (bench option) — e.g. an aespa / IVE-class pair
Mirror of B for girl groups. Similar trade-off profile (good C3/C5, harder C4). Included as a
bench option; **documented-toxicity evidence is thinner** than A or B, so C1/C7 need more
verification before it could be a finalist.

## 4. The core trade-off
> **Flagship (A)** maximizes toxicity magnitude, size, and documentation — but its rivalry is
> **diffuse (cross-lane) and famously public** (demand-characteristics risk, dual-stan overlap).
> **Same-lane (B)** gives **cleaner, head-to-head competitive toxicity, lower dual-stan overlap,
> and lower demand risk** — at the cost of **scale and recruitment ease.**

This — magnitude/power (A) vs. mechanistic cleanliness (B) — is the decision the team is actually
making.

---

## 5. The multi-stan threat to the C3 screen
`PLAN.md` §7 requires participants who are **single-fandom, not strong dual-fans** — otherwise the
rivalry isn't real *for them* and the manipulation has nothing to recategorize. Survey data makes
this a live recruitment constraint:

> A K-Pop Radar survey (2023, N≈1,085) estimated **~75.5% of international fans are "multi-stans"**,
> versus **~60% of Korean fans being single-group stans.**

Implications:
- **(a)** The "not a strong dual-fan" screen will **exclude a large fraction of international
  recruits** — budget for a **low screening pass-through rate** and inflate the recruitment target.
- **(b)** **Domestic / Korean-leaning recruitment** yields cleaner single-fandom cells (but trades
  against language/panel logistics and the age-verification burden).
- **(c)** **Same-lane direct rivals (B)** naturally carry **lower mutual dual-stanning** than a
  big-two that many people both admire (A) — a concrete point in **B's favor for C3**.

## 6. The pilot that decides (implements `PLAN.md` §13.1)
Before locking, run a **short screening survey** to N fans of each candidate fandom measuring:
1. **Superordinate registration (the gate).** Does "K-pop fan" register as a real, shared in-group
   for members of *both* fandoms? (CIIM common-in-group-identity baseline item — the same
   instrument used at T0 in the main study.)
2. **Felt rivalry / animosity** toward the other fandom (a short feeling-thermometer + one
   social-distance item — the DV-adjacent check that toxicity has something to bite on).
3. **Within-pair dual-stan rate** (the C3 check): what fraction stan *both* candidate groups?

**Decision rule.** Prefer the pair that **maximizes (felt rivalry × superordinate registration)
while keeping the dual-stan rate low.** A pair that fails the superordinate-registration gate is
disqualified **regardless** of how toxic it is — that is the §13.1 gating logic.

**Scope.** Run **two finalists** through this pilot: one **flagship (A)** and one **same-lane (B)**.

---

## 7. Recommendation
- **Finalists to pilot:** **A (ARMY × BLINK)** and **one same-lane B pair** (lead candidate:
  **STAY × CARAT**; alternate: **Treasure × ENGENE**).
- **Lean:** **B is the cleaner instrument** for isolating the CIIM mechanism (better C3, C5, and
  lower demand-characteristics). If B's pilot shows the superordinate registers strongly enough and
  felt rivalry is high, **prefer B**. Keep **A as the high-power fallback** if B's rivalry reads too
  mild or its cells can't be recruited to balance.
- This is a recommendation — the **final call is the team's, decided by the pilot numbers**, not by
  this doc.

- **Public demo now uses the real names (ARMY / BLINK).** *(Superseded — see the DECISION banner

  (derive from the *chosen* pair's discourse), and §11 age verification.
